Abstract

Reconstructing 3D objects from a single image remains challenging due to severe viewpoint ambiguity and inconsistent multi-view supervision. Existing image-to-3D methods rely on generative models to infer unseen views, but often suffer from unstable geometry and cross-view consistency. In this paper, we propose GenerativeGaussian, an image-to-3D reconstruction framework based on 3D Gaussian Splatting and diffusion priors. We reformulate SDS-based optimization as a view-consistent geometric learning problem and introduce a perceptual geometry supervision with a nearest-anchor strategy to stabilize diffusion-guided Gaussian optimization. In addition, we incorporate a depth regularization scheme to constrain the spatial distribution of Gaussian primitives and improve geometric fidelity. To further enhance appearance quality, we extract a textured mesh and refine the UV texture using the color and structural constraints. Extensive experiments demonstrate that GenerativeGaussian outperforms state-of-the-art image-to-3D methods in color fidelity, geometric accuracy, and multi-view consistency.
